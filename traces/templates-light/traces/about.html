{% trans "About Biketory" %}

{% blocktrans %}Biketory is a web application that allows cyclists to import their GPX traces and visualise the closed geographic surfaces covered on an interactive map.{% endblocktrans %}

{% trans "How it works" %}

{% blocktrans %}Upload your GPX files, and Biketory automatically detects closed loops in your routes. These surfaces are then displayed on a map and divided into hexagons to track your progress.{% endblocktrans %}

{% trans "Hexagons" %}

{% blocktrans %}The map is divided into hexagons. Each time one of your rides crosses a hexagon, it becomes acquired and your score on that hexagon increases.{% endblocktrans %}

{% blocktrans %}When your score on a hexagon is the highest among all users, that hexagon becomes conquered. Other riders can take it back by riding through it more times than you — so keep pedalling!{% endblocktrans %}

{% trans "The story" %}

{% blocktrans %}Biketory was born from the passion of a cycling enthusiast who never tires of exploring new gravel paths. Setting off on an adventure, discovering an unknown trail, riding for the sheer joy of riding — that is the spirit of the project. The idea was simple: have a fun way to visualise all the ground covered over your rides, and turn each loop into a small victory on the map.{% endblocktrans %}

{% trans "Source code" %}

{% blocktrans %}Biketory is an open source project. The code is available on GitHub.{% endblocktrans %}
